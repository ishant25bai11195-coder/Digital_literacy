# UPI / Online Payment Fraud – Case Study

## 📌 Overview
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India. In this type of fraud, scammers trick users into sending money or sharing sensitive information such as OTPs or UPI PINs.

## ⚙️ How the Scam Works (Step-by-Step)
1. The victim receives a call/message from someone pretending to be a bank official or buyer.
2. The scammer sends a payment request or QR code.
3. The victim is asked to scan the QR code or approve the request.
4. The victim enters their UPI PIN, thinking they will receive money.
5. Instead, the money is transferred to the scammer’s account.

## 🧾 Real-Life Example (OLX Scam)
In many real cases, sellers on platforms like OLX are contacted by fake buyers. The fraudster agrees to purchase the item and sends a QR code, claiming it is needed to transfer money. When the seller scans the QR code and enters their UPI PIN, money is deducted instead of being received. Many users have lost money due to this misunderstanding.

## 🎯 Who is Targeted?
- Students and young users  
- Online sellers  
- People new to digital payments  

## ⚠️ Consequences
- Financial loss  
- Emotional stress  
- Misuse of personal information  

## 💡 Conclusion
This case study highlights the importance of understanding how UPI transactions work. Users should be cautious and never enter their UPI PIN unless they are making a payment.
